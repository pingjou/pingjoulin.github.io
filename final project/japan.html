Tamagoyaki

Ingredients

  • eggs
  • seasoning sauce
  • oil

Step

  1. Add the eggs, water, and memmi seasoning sauce into a pourable cup and beat well with a whisk until fully combined. Heat a tamagoyaki pan over medium heat and apply a bit of oil.
  2. Pour a thin layer of the egg mixture and scramble for the first few seconds.
  3. Allow the egg to set in an even layer until it's about 70% cooked and the surface is still wet.
  4. Roll up the egg into an omelet and set it to one side of the pan. Lightly oil the pan and pour another thin layer of egg mixture into the pan.
  5. Lift the omelet to allow the egg mixture to flow under it. Slightly scramble the eggs for a few seconds and allow them to set into an even layer. Pop large bubbles as you see them for even cooking.
  6. When the egg mixture is about 70% cooked but the surface is still wet, gently roll the omelet to the other side of the pan.
  7. Continue cooking and rolling each layer until the egg mixture is used up.
  8. Remove the tamagoyaki from the pan and allow it to rest on a cutting board to get clean slices. When it's warm to the touch, slice into thick slices.

Okonomiyaki

Ingredients

  • Nagaimo
  • Tenkasu
  • Okonomiyaki Sauce
  • Pork / Bacon
  • Cabbage
  • Japanese Mayonnaise
  • Katsuobushi (Dried Bonito Flakes)
  • Aonori (Dried Green Seaweed)

Step

  1. Fry the bacon until cooked through and crispy, then set aside to
    cool.
  2. To make the batter, combine the eggs and dashi powder dissolved in
    the warm water in a small mixing bowl and beat gently and then
    pour over with the egg and dashi stock mixture.
    Stir and combine until smooth
  3. Add the shredded cabbage, half of the green onion slices, cooked
    bacon and corn into the batter mixture. Mix until all the dry
    ingredients are evenly coated (but don't overmix,
    we want to keep that cabbage nice and fluffy).
  4. Scoop out the mixture onto a large frying pan over medium heat.
    We like aiming for a circle shape about 10 cm / 4 inches wide.
    Cook each pancake for a few mins per side, until
    lightly browned on both sides.
  5. Transfer your freshly cooked pancakes to serving plates, then top
    with drizzles of kewpie mayonnaise and okonomiyaki sauce.
    Add top with sprinklings of dried seaweed flakes,
    bonito flakes and a few slices of green onion to taste.

Oyakodon

Ingredients

  • 200g chicken breast or boneless chicken thigh
  • half an onion
  • 2 eggs
  • white rice
  • water 150c
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp rice wine
  • 2 tbsp mirin
  • 1 tbsp sugar

Step

  1. Chop the chicken into proper size and the onion into strips, stir the sauce ingredients evenly and set aside.
  2. Put the sauce, chicken and onion in the pot and boil until the chicken is fully cooked and the onion becomes transparent.
  3. Whisk the eggs lightly in a bowl, the whites and yolks need not be fully mixed.
  4. Pour over the egg wash, cover the pot and cook on high heat for 10-20 seconds.
  5. Spread on the fragrant white rice, the Oyakodon is ready.

Takoyaki

Ingredients

  • ¼ cup katsuobushi
  • 2 green onions/scallions
  • 1 tbsp pickled red ginger
  • 4.2 oz octopus sashimi
  • 2 tbsp neutral-flavored oil
  • ⅓ cup tenkasu/agedama
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp Diamond Crystal kosher salt2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • 1½ cups dashi
  • ½ cup Takoyaki Sauce
  • Japanese mayonnaise
  • katsuobushi
  • aonori

Step

  1. Grind the katsuobushi into a fine powder and set aside
  2. Cut the green onions into fine slices, mince the red pickled ginger ,and the octopus into small size.
  3. Combine them all-purpose flour, baking powder, and kosher salt and whisk it all together, adding the eggs, soy sauce, and dashi.
  4. Whisk it all together until well blended and transfer the batter to a measuring cup with a handle.
  5. Heat the takoyaki pan to 400ºF (200ºC) over medium heat. Using a brush, generously oil the pan's rounded chambers and flat surface on top.
  6. When you see smoke rising from the pan, pour the batter to fill the chambers.
  7. Add 1-3 octopus pieces,sprinkle the tenkasu, green onion, and pickled red ginger on top.
  8. After 3 minutes,use skewers to break the connected batter between each ball.
  9. Rotate each piece to connected dough back into the ball as you turn to help form a ball shape for 4 minutes
